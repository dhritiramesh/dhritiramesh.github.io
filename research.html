Evaluating LLaMA Models and Prompt Engineering for Educational Applications

Colorado School of Mines • 2024-Present

Large Language Models Prompt Engineering Education ACM SIGCSE 2026

Explored prompt engineering strategies for Meta's LLaMA models to improve reliability, reproducibility, and latency in AI-assisted educational applications. This work was presented at the ACM SIGCSE Technical Symposium 2026 and published as a peer-reviewed first-author paper in the ACM Digital Library.

Abstract

This project evaluates prompt engineering strategies for large language models through an analysis of response accuracy, reproducibility, and latency across multiple model variants. The study consisted of 174 prompt-response trials spanning ten academic categories and compared three versions of Meta's LLaMA models (1B, 3B, and 8B parameters). Results demonstrated a clear trade-off between reasoning performance and computational efficiency: larger models consistently produced more accurate and reproducible responses but required significantly longer inference times. These findings provide practical guidance for selecting and deploying LLMs in educational settings, where balancing response quality with speed is essential for effective AI-assisted learning.

Publication

  • First-author, peer-reviewed publication in the ACM Digital Library.
  • Presented at the ACM SIGCSE Technical Symposium 2026.
  • Discussed the work with educators and AI researchers from around the world, whose feedback is guiding the translation of this research into a practical educational tool.

Orbital Determination of Mars Crossing Asteroid 5066 Garradd

Summer Science Program (SSP) • 2025

Calculated the orbital elements of a Mars-crossing asteroid using real telescope data and Python-based numerical methods in collaboration with peers and professors.

Abstract

This project discusses the process used to study the motion of the Mars-crossing Asteroid 5066 Garradd (1990 MA) and the process used to redetermine and confirm its orbit. The study included determining the asteroid's position on five observation nights spread throughout the months of June and July 2025. Using the positions of 5066 Garradd during three of these observations, the position (with respect to the Sun) and velocity vectors were calculated via the Method of Gauss. The six orbital elements and corresponding uncertainties were computed using the Monte Carlo method. These elements were later used to determine the asteroid's orbit, with consistency checks confirming an accurate ephemeris for the remaining two observations.

Journey

This project was made possible through my participation in the Summer Science Program (SSP), where I collaborated with peers from around the world and mentors in astronomy and physics. I learned celestial mechanics, orbital dynamics, and applied advanced mathematics using Python scripts for data analysis and orbit simulation — demonstrating how computer science can advance research in astrophysics.

Anemia Early Detection App

Congressional App Challenge Submission • 2025

Developed a mobile app integrating a CNN model to detect anemia risk from non-invasive conjunctiva images, enhancing accessibility to early screening tools.

Overview

Developed a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model and mobile application designed to detect potential signs of anemia through non-invasive image analysis of the human conjunctiva (the inner eyelid). The app processes user-submitted images to assess pallor and color distribution, providing a probability-based estimate of anemia risk. This project aims to make early screening more accessible, particularly in regions lacking medical infrastructure or diagnostic tools and especially for young women who are especially susceptible to anemia.

Journey

This project stemmed from my passion for combining computer science and healthcare to solve real-world problems. Working with a peer, we trained a CNN model using a curated dataset of conjunctiva images to recognize color variations linked to anemia. Alongside developing the app interface, I collaborated with mentors in both medicine and machine learning to ensure accurate model use. Currently, the app is being evaluated by the Congressional App Challenge, though it remains unpublished due to medical data collection confidentiality concerns.
