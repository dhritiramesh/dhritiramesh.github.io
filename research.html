Evaluating LLaMA Models and Prompt Engineering for Educational Applications

Colorado School of Mines • 2024-Present

Large Language Models Prompt Engineering Education Association of Computing Machinery (ACM)

Explored prompt engineering strategies for Meta's LLaMA models to improve reliability, reproducibility, and latency in AI-assisted educational applications. This work was presented at the ACM SIGCSE Technical Symposium 2026 and published as a peer-reviewed first-author paper in the ACM Digital Library.

Abstract

This project evaluates prompt engineering strategies for large language models through an analysis of response accuracy, reproducibility, and latency across multiple model variants. The study consisted of 174 prompt-response trials spanning ten academic categories and compared three versions of Meta's LLaMA models (1B, 3B, and 8B parameters). Results demonstrated a clear trade-off between reasoning performance and computational efficiency: larger models consistently produced more accurate and reproducible responses but required significantly longer inference times. These findings provide practical guidance for selecting and deploying LLMs in educational settings, where balancing response quality with speed is essential for effective AI-assisted learning.

  • Presented at the ACM SIGCSE Technical Symposium 2026.
  • Discussed the work with educators and AI researchers from around the world, whose feedback is guiding the translation of this research into a practical educational tool.

Orbital Determination of Mars-Crossing Asteroid 5066 Garradd

Summer Science Program (SSP) • 2025

Astrophysics Orbital Mechanics Python Data Analysis

Determined and validated the orbit of the Mars-crossing asteroid 5066 Garradd using observational telescope data, mathematical processes, and computational techniques. Collaborated with two fellow researchers and faculty mentors through the Summer Science Program to solve a real orbital mechanics problem.

Abstract

This project focused on redetermining and validating the orbit of the Mars-crossing asteroid 5066 Garradd (1990 MA). Using five nights of telescope observations collected throughout June and July 2025, our team calculated the asteroid's position and velocity vectors using the Method of Gauss before determining its six orbital elements and associated uncertainties through Monte Carlo analysis. The resulting orbital solution was validated by accurately predicting the asteroid's position during two additional observation nights, demonstrating the effectiveness of computational methods for orbital determination.

AI-Powered Anemia Early Detection Platform

Independent Project • 2025-Present

Healthcare AI Computer Vision CNNs Web Development

Co-developed a web-based platform that uses computer vision to estimate anemia risk from non-invasive conjunctiva images. The project combines deep learning with accessible screening methods and is being refined through feedback from medical professionals.

Overview

This project explores how convolutional neural networks (CNNs) can support early anemia screening through image analysis of the palpebral conjunctiva (the inner eyelid). Working with a peer, I developed a web application that analyzes uploaded images to estimate anemia risk based on learned visual features associated with conjunctival pallor. The long-term goal is to create an accessible, non-invasive screening tool that can complement traditional testing, particularly in underserved communities where laboratory diagnostics may be limited.

Resources

  • Co-developed with a peer.
  • Currently being refined with feedback from physicians and medical professionals to improve usability, before working on making the platform more broadly available.
